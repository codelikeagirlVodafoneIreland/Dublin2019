Amur Leopards

Weight: Males generally weigh 32-48 kg, but can weigh up to 75 kg. Females are smaller than the males at 25-43 kg.




Description: The Amur leopard is adapted to the cool climate by having thick fur which grows up to 7.5 cm long in winter. For camouflage in the snow, their coat is paler than other leopard subspecies.




Prey: Amur leopards hunt a very wide variety of animals including roe deer, sika deer, badgers and hares.




Poaching: Poaching of both leopards and prey species is a serious threat.
